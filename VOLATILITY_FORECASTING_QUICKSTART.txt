🎨 VOLATILITY REGIMES (Quick Reference)
================================================================================

VERY LOW (0-0.5%)
🟢 Market: Extremely calm
💰 Options: Very cheap
📈 Strategy: SELL options (collect premium)
⚠️  Risk: LOW

LOW (0.5-1.0%)
🟢 Market: Calm
💰 Options: Cheap
📈 Strategy: SELL strangles/straddles
⚠️  Risk: LOW-MEDIUM

NORMAL (1.0-1.5%)
🟡 Market: Typical
💰 Options: Fair value
📈 Strategy: Balanced (buy or sell)
⚠️  Risk: MEDIUM

ELEVATED (1.5-2.5%)
🟡 Market: Increased uncertainty
💰 Options: Getting expensive
📈 Strategy: Reduce positions
⚠️  Risk: MEDIUM-HIGH

HIGH (2.5-4.0%)
🔴 Market: High volatility
💰 Options: Expensive
📈 Strategy: BUY options, avoid selling
⚠️  Risk: HIGH

EXTREME (>4.0%)
🔴 Market: Crisis mode
💰 Options: Very expensive
📈 Strategy: Protective only
⚠️  Risk: EXTREME

🔍 HV vs VIX INTERPRETATION
================================================================================

HV > VIX (Positive Spread)
💡 Meaning: Realized vol > Expected vol
💰 Options: UNDERPRICED (cheap)
📈 Action: Consider BUYING options
Example: HV=18%, VIX=13% → Buy options

VIX > HV (Negative Spread)
💡 Meaning: Expected vol > Realized vol
💰 Options: OVERPRICED (expensive)
📉 Action: Consider SELLING options
Example: VIX=20%, HV=15% → Sell options

HV ≈ VIX (Aligned)
💡 Meaning: Expectations match reality
💰 Options: FAIRLY PRICED
↔️  Action: Neutral, use other factors
Example: HV=15%, VIX=15% → Fair value

💰 OPTIONS STRATEGY SELECTION
================================================================================

WHEN VOLATILITY IS LOW:
✅ Sell Strangles (OTM calls + OTM puts)
✅ Sell Straddles (ATM calls + ATM puts)
✅ Iron Condors (defined risk)
✅ Credit Spreads (bull/bear)
❌ Avoid buying options (expensive time decay)

WHEN VOLATILITY IS NORMAL:
✅ Vertical Spreads (bull/bear)
✅ Calendar Spreads (time decay)
✅ Butterfly Spreads (neutral)
✅ Balanced approach

WHEN VOLATILITY IS HIGH:
✅ Buy Options (volatility expansion)
✅ Debit Spreads (defined risk)
✅ Protective Puts (insurance)
✅ Long Straddles (big move expected)
❌ Avoid selling naked options
